## ðŸŽ¯ Types of Trading Strategies

Different strategies suit different personalities and time commitments:

### 1. Day Trading
Making multiple trades within a single day to profit from short-term price movements.

**Pros:**
- Quick potential profits
- No overnight risk

**Cons:**
- Requires constant monitoring
- High stress and time commitment

### 2. Swing Trading
Holding positions for days or weeks to capture medium-term trends.

**Pros:**
- Less time-intensive than day trading
- Captures larger price movements

**Cons:**
- Overnight/weekend risk exposure
- Requires patience

### 3. HODLing (Long-term Investing)
Buying and holding for months or years.

**Pros:**
- Minimal time commitment
- Historically profitable for major coins

**Cons:**
- Capital locked for extended periods
- Must endure major drawdowns

## âš ï¸ Risk Management Essentials

**The most important skill in trading is protecting your capital.**

### Golden Rules:

1. **Never invest more than you can afford to lose**
2. **Use stop-loss orders** to limit potential losses
3. **Diversify** across multiple assets
4. **Start small** and scale up as you gain experience
5. **Keep emotions in check** - fear and greed are your enemies

### Position Sizing Formula

A common approach is the 1-2% rule:
- Risk only 1-2% of your total portfolio on any single trade
- This ensures no single loss can devastate your account

## ðŸ“ˆ Reading Charts Like a Pro

Understanding candlestick patterns is fundamental:

### Basic Patterns

**Bullish Patterns (Price likely to rise):**
- Hammer
- Morning Star
- Bullish Engulfing

**Bearish Patterns (Price likely to fall):**
- Shooting Star
- Evening Star
- Bearish Engulfing

### Support and Resistance

- **Support**: Price level where buying pressure exceeds selling
- **Resistance**: Price level where selling pressure exceeds buying

These levels often act as psychological barriers where price tends to bounce or reverse.
